# Worker Taxonomy Tree

Below is a node tree representation of the concepts related to what a worker needs to be able to find solutions and solve problems.

```
Worker
├── Intent
│   ├── Vision — An aspirational depiction of a desired future state that provides long-term orientation and purpose.
│   ├── Goal — A specific, measurable outcome that an agent aims to achieve within a defined scope.
│   ├── Objective — A concrete, time-bound milestone that advances progress toward a broader goal.
│   └── Desired Outcomes — The observable states or conditions that signify successful resolution of a problem or completion of effort.
├── Direction
│   ├── Approach — A general manner or angle of tackling a problem, defining the overarching stance taken.
│   ├── Strategy — A deliberate plan of action that allocates resources and sequences efforts to achieve a goal.
│   └── Principles — Foundational rules or values that constrain and guide decision-making regardless of context.
├── Guidance
│   ├── Framework — A structured set of interrelated components that organizes thinking and action within a domain.
│   ├── Patterns — Reusable, proven solutions to recurring problems or structures that can be adapted to new situations.
│   ├── Architecture — The high-level organization and structuring of components and their relationships within a system.
│   └── Models — Simplified representations of reality that capture essential features for reasoning and communication.
├── Methodology
│   ├── Rules — Explicit, prescriptive statements that define what is required, permitted, or forbidden.
│   ├── Methods — Systematic, ordered procedures for accomplishing a task or producing a result.
│   └── Processes — A connected series of actions, events, or steps that transform inputs into outputs.
└── Skills
    ├── Tactic — A specific, context-dependent action taken to address an immediate situation or opportunity.
    ├── Technique — A refined, repeatable way of performing a specific task that improves effectiveness or efficiency.
    ├── Practice — The habitual, deliberate application of methods and techniques to develop and maintain proficiency.
    ├── Heuristic — A practical rule-of-thumb or mental shortcut that guides action when full information is unavailable.
    └── Tool Use — The ability to select, operate, and leverage external instruments to extend capabilities.
