# Interview Question Bank

Questions organized by topic. Ask sequentially, one at a time. Adapt based on answers.

## 1. Problem & Motivation

- What problem does this initiative solve?
- Who experiences this problem today? How do they cope with it now?
- Why is this important to solve now? What changed?
- What happens if we don't solve this?
- Is there a specific trigger or event that prompted this initiative?

## 2. Users & Personas

- Who are the primary users of this capability?
- Are there secondary users or stakeholders who are affected?
- What does a typical user's workflow look like today (before this feature)?
- What does their workflow look like after this feature ships?
- Are there different user types with different needs? Describe each.

## 3. Scope & Boundaries

- What is the core of this initiative — the minimum that must work?
- What is explicitly out of scope for this initiative?
- Are there adjacent features or systems this touches but does not change?
- Is there a "phase 2" that people might confuse with phase 1? Clarify the boundary.
- Are there existing features this replaces, deprecates, or builds on top of?

## 4. Success Criteria

- How will we know this initiative succeeded? What does "done and working" look like?
- Are there measurable metrics (performance targets, adoption rates, error rates)?
- What would make a user say "this is great"?
- What would make a user say "this is broken"?
- Are there non-functional requirements (speed, reliability, security, accessibility)?

## 5. High-Level Capabilities

- What are the major sub-features or capabilities within this initiative?
- For each capability: what does it do, who uses it, and what is the expected behavior?
- Do any of these capabilities depend on each other? Describe the dependency.
- Are any of these capabilities reusable across other parts of the product?
- Which capability is the most complex? Which is the most valuable?

## 6. Technical Approach

- What is the tech stack for this project? (Languages, frameworks, databases, infrastructure)
- Are there existing systems, APIs, or components this should integrate with?
- Are there architectural patterns or conventions this should follow?
- Are there third-party libraries, services, or tools that should be used or avoided?
- Are there performance, scalability, or security requirements that shape the technical approach?
- What data does this feature need? Where does it come from? Where does it go?

## 7. Risks & Open Questions

- What are the biggest risks to this initiative succeeding?
- What technical uncertainties exist? (Unproven libraries, unknown performance characteristics)
- What product uncertainties exist? (Unvalidated assumptions about user behavior)
- Are there dependencies on other teams, systems, or decisions?
- What questions are still open that need to be resolved before implementation?

## 8. Phasing & Rollout

- Should this be delivered all at once or in phases?
- If phased, what is the MVP? What comes next?
- Are there milestones or checkpoints that matter?
- Does this need a rollout plan (feature flags, gradual release, beta testing)?
- Are there launch dependencies (documentation, marketing, training, support)?

## Follow-Up Prompts (use when answers are vague)

- "Can you give me a concrete example?"
- "What does that look like from the user's perspective?"
- "How would you describe that to someone who doesn't know the system?"
- "Is there a specific scenario you have in mind?"
- "What would be the simplest version of that?"
- "What could go wrong with that approach?"
- "How does that compare to what exists today?"
